Take More Control of Your Energy Bill

Shift & Save Pilot Program

Congratulations! You’ve been chosen for the Shift & Save Pilot Program – a new approach to pricing energy that puts YOU more in control.

Rather than paying the same rate for electricity regardless of time or day, Shift & Save gives you the option to simply shift usage away from 3 p.m. to 7 p.m. weekdays - and save by taking advantage of lower rates.

Get Started

Choose the Hours You Use Power

You make the choices that best fit your household and your budget. A few simple changes can make a difference. When you run your dishwasher, air conditioner or clothes washer before 3 p.m. and after 7 p.m., for example, you’ll pay less for electricity.

Shift away from Peak Hours for a lower rate.

Peak Hours are 3 p.m. to 7 p.m., Monday–Friday, year-round.

Shift & Save puts you in control when you know about Peak Hours and Off-Peak Hours.

  • Peak Hours – 3:00 p.m. to 7:00 p.m. every weekday – when electricity demand and prices are at their highest.
  • Off-Peak Hours – Pay less for electricity after 7:00 p.m. and before 3:00 p.m. weekdays and all hours on the weekend.

What's in it for you?

Shift & Save is a voluntary pilot program approved by the State ofMichigan to help better manage demand on the energy grid, protect our environment and give you more control of your bill. Simple changes in your schedule can save you money. We want you to succeed, so we’ll provide you with tips, tools and incentives along the way. How it worksYour electricity use is measured in kilowatts and you are charged a kilowatts-per-hour (kWh) basis. That rate is lower during Off-Peak Hours. It’s also different in summer months (June through September) than it is the rest of the year (October through May), as you can see in the illustration below.

Savings Tips

Appliances

Run your dishwasher, air conditioner and clothes washer before 3 p.m. and after 7 p.m. to lower your bill.

Cooling Your Home

Begin cooling your home before Peak Hours begin at 3 p.m. on weekdays. Then, raise the temperature by just four degrees during the Peak period. You’ll stay cool and save.

Heating Your Home

Set your thermostat between 68 and 70 degrees when you're home and at 65 degrees or lower when you're away. You can save around 3% on heating costs for each degree you lower your thermostat.

Make a Habit of Saving

Unplug non-essential appliances when you’re not using them. They draw power just by being plugged in. You can reduce up to 10% of electricity use by unplugging appliances and devices when you’re not using them.

Lighting

Install LED bulbs to save energy and money. ENERGY STAR®-certified lighting uses up to 90% less energy than traditional incandescent bulbs.

Dishwasher

Run full loads in your dishwasher. About the same amount of energy and water are used in each wash, regardless of the load size.

Programmable Thermostat

Install a programmable thermostat to automatically adjustyour home temperatureduring Peak and Off-Peak Hours.

Clothes Dryer

Fill up your clothes dryer, but don't overload it. The dryer needs space for air circulation to efficiently dry fabrics. And did you know that a longer drying cycle on a low heat setting uses less energy?

Clothes Washer

Use the cold water setting whenever possible. Heating the water can account for up to 90% of the energy it takes to operate a clothes washer.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Shift & Save pilot program?
Shift & Save is a pilot program approved by the State of Michigan to help better manage demand on the energy grid, protect our environment and give you more control of your bill. The information and knowledge gained from the study will help us design future rate plans.
Will I save money on Shift & Save?
It’s up to you! You can save money by shifting some of your energy usage away from the hours of 3 p.m. – 7 p.m. weekdays. Shift & Save is a pilot program based on how much electricity you use and when you use it, giving you more control of your bill.
Do Shift & Save rates vary at different times of the year?
Yes, there are Summer and Non-Summer seasons. Summer begins on June 1 and runs through the end of September. Non-Summer is October through May. Rates also differ on weekdays between Peak Hours (3 p.m. – 7 p.m.) and Off-Peak Hours (all the time outside of 3 p.m. – 7 p.m. weekdays).
Is Shift & Save voluntary? How long is my commitment?
The Shift & Save program is voluntary and will run for 12 months, beginning May 2020. At the end of the pilot, you will stay on your new Shift & Save rate unless you choose to opt out, which you may do at any time.
How does the Shift & Save survey program work?
When you participate in Shift & Save you will receive three brief surveys during the one-year pilot program. You will get $75 in total incentives for completing the surveys. You must remain on the Shift & Save pilot rate through April 2021 in order to receive all three surveys.
When will my new rate take effect?
Your new rate will take effect on the first day of your May 2020 billing cycle. For example, if you enroll April 28 and your next billing cycle begins on May 15, your Shift & Save rate will take effect May 15.
What shifts in behavior are most likely to reduce my bill under this program?
Shifting use of your most energy-hungry appliances away from 3 p.m. - 7 p.m. on weekdays will help you maximize your monthly savings. Those appliances include your clothes washer, dishwasher, air conditioner, dehumidifier, and oven. Check outthe Savings Tips part of this site for more information on how you can get the most out of Shift & Save.
Do other energy companies offer plans like Shift & Save?
Yes. Pilot programs like Shift & Save are offered on a voluntary basis in nearly every state
Does DTE have tools I can use to monitor my energy use?

Yes! This May, you’ll have access to three tools that will specifically help you manage your usage under your ACPP rate:

  • Alerts to notify you when you’re at risk of exceeding your targeted energy budget
  • Real-time usage graphs to keep you on track through Summer Months
  • Rate calculator to help you make smart decisions that lower your bill

In the meantime, use our free Insight app and Energy Bridge to monitor your energyuse in real-time. As a pilot participant, you will receive a free, one-year subscription to the Energy Bridge.

Do I need to install additional equipment in my home in order to participate in Shift & Save?

No additional equipment is required.

To help you better manage your energy use and maximize your savings, you will be eligible to receive a one-year subscription to DTE’s Energy Bridge at no cost while enrolled in Shift & Save.

What are my options if I decide I don’t want to complete the pilot?

Opt-in group: There are no penalties for opting out. If you enroll and do not wish tocomplete the pilot, you may unenroll at any time by visiting this landing page or by calling 855-921-1852.

How do I know if I qualify for the Shift & Save Pilot Program?

The Shift & Save Pilot Program is available to Full Service residential customers currently served on DTE’s Residential Service rate D-1 with no more than one premise at their service address. Shift & Save cannot be combined with any other tariff, rider, payment plan, pilot, separately metered service or special provision. What this means for you is that if you decide you’d like to enroll in one of DTE’s other pilot or payment programs, such as Budget Wise Billing, Smart Savers or Electric Choice, you’ll need to opt out of Shift & Save to do so. Still not sure if you’reeligible for Shift & Save? Reach us at 855.921.1852.
